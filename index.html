The Methodology

The Scientific Creative Framework

I don't spray and pray. If you can't tell WHAT you're testing and WHY before you launch an ad, don't even bother testing it. Here are the fundamental beliefs that drive my media buying and creative architecture.

🧪

Science > Guessing

Guessing teaches you nothing. Every test must be documented so when an ad wins, it can be replicated, and when it fails, the trap can be avoided. We are optimizing, not gambling.

⚡ View The Process ▼

Hypothesis-Driven Testing: Every ad needs a specific hypothesis (e.g., "We're testing problem-aware messaging because our product-aware audience is saturated.")

The 3-Step Process: 1. Research the market edge. 2. Choose one variable to test and plan hooks. 3. Launch in one ad set and see where Meta allocates the budget.

The AI Trap: Letting junior strategists use AI to write copy before producing winners manually kills their development. Do the manual reps to build judgment, then use AI to scale.

🌪️

Funnel Psychology

We must map our creative to the customer's exact mindset, guiding prospects from Unaware to Purchase like a binge-worthy Netflix series.

⚡ View Funnel Mechanics ▼

The High-Friction CTA: "Sign Up" feels like a massive commitment. Fix: Use low-commitment triggers ("Watch Video") and drive traffic to a VSL landing page before asking for a call.

The 5 Awareness Levels (Schwartz):

  • Unaware: Educate & Agitate (Largest scale potential).
  • Problem-Aware: Mirror their Reality.
  • Solution-Aware: Disqualify old methods.
  • Product-Aware: Position against alternatives.
  • Brand-Aware: Social Proof & Urgency (Sales).
📈

Media Buying Architecture

A lean, efficient account structure requires better creatives, not more ad account hacking. Stop fighting "Ad Fatigue" and start solving the "Ad Cap."

⚡ View Account Structure ▼

The CBO Structure: 1 CBO for Testing & Scaling. 5-10 active ad sets (1 concept each). 3-5 hook variations per set. $10-$20 minimum daily budget per set to force spend across all concepts.

Ad Fatigue vs. Ad Cap: Ads rarely "fatigue." They hit an Ad Cap based on the specific audience pocket that resonates with that exact visual. The fix isn't fighting fatigue; it's creating new ads to resonate with OTHER people so you can spend more without hitting audience limits.

✍️

Copy & Cognitive Biases

Applying direct-response psychological triggers to make ad copy print. Stop making baseless claims; give the brain reasons to believe.

⚡ View Bias Tactics ▼

Specificity Bias: Specific numbers are more believable (15,363+ > 15,000).

Authority: Stop making baseless claims; let an authority say it ("Dermatologists approve...").

Negation Bias: A negative message creates a negative mental image. Use positive framing ("Sleep like a baby" > "Never struggle with insomnia again").

Humor Effect: Funny copy makes brands trustworthy and lowers the buyer's guard.

The Truth Effect: Shorter, punchier sentences are processed faster and believed more easily.
